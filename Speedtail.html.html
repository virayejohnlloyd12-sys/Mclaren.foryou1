OVERVIEW

With a design that channels uninterrupted airflow from nose to tail, the McLaren Speedtail is a fusion of science and art – the epitome of minimalist elegance. It was born from a single-minded vision to create the most aerodynamically efficient car possible.

True to McLaren's design philosophy, the distinctive elongated tail was created for a reason. Instead of a traditional rear wing, the McLaren Speedtail's trailing edge contains ailerons – components usually found on aircraft, which extend at high speeds to provide balance, as well as an airbrake function. Unlike traditional hinged ailerons, however, these are formed from the carbon fibre bodywork, which bends to increase downforce. The very body of the car changes shape as it glides through the air.

DRAMATIC EXPRESSION

The McLaren Speedtail encapsulates pure McLaren drama. At the time of its release, it delivered the fastest acceleration of any of our creations – 300 kph (186 mph) in just 12.8 seconds. Its top speed of 403kph (250 mph) outstripped even that of the legendary McLaren F1.

This is the combined result of a stunning aerodynamic design, a dry weight of only 1,430 kg (3,153 lbs) and the immense force of the 1,050 PS (1,036 bhp) petrol-electric hybrid powertrain. An elegant, aerodynamic wonder – with fire at its core.
